Making an RL Agent Trade Power Like a Real Market Player

MDP modeling, POMDP reality, and an RL agent trying to make money in between.

What I'm Actually Building

A reinforcement learning agent that participates in ISO day-ahead electricity markets. It looks at historical load, price signals, and forecasts, learns how to place bids, and tries to make economically sensible decisions instead of just doing math tricks in a vacuum.

We model the market as an MDP for tractability, even though real electricity markets behave much closer to a POMDP — partial observability, noisy forecasts, and strategic participants included. The agent builds its state from demand and price signals, selects hourly bidding strategies, and updates its policy based on realized profit. Under the hood

  • Discretized state + action spaces so the agent doesn't panic
  • Real load + price data instead of synthetic textbook examples
  • Reward shaped around profit, risk penalties, and market realism
  • Training until it stops behaving like a complete toddler

What We Care About

We're not just asking whether the agent converges... we evaluate whether the learned policy is profitable, stable, and economically rational under realistic market conditions.

Reinforcement learning agents are notoriously easy to “look good” in simulation while learning brittle or unrealistic policies. To avoid this, we benchmark against baseline strategies, monitor policy stability throughout training, and analyze whether the agent's behavior aligns with real economic intuition rather than exploiting artifacts of the environment.

We also track the change in Q-values across episodes as a proxy for learning dynamics — large early updates followed by stabilization suggest the policy is converging toward a consistent strategy rather than oscillating between behaviors.

Metrics include:

  • Profit relative to baseline strategies
  • Policy stability during training
  • Convergence behavior
  • Economic plausiblity of bidding decisions
